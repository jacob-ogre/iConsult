About iConsult

Section 7 of the U.S. Endangered Species Act (ESA) directs federal agencies to use their authorities to conserve threatened and endangered species. One way they do that is by "consulting" with the U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service (FWS) and/or the National Marine Fisheries Service (NMFS) if an action the agency will carry out, permit, or fund might harm ESA-listed species.

While the Services have web apps for consultations, they are dated (PCTS for NMFS) or very spotty in their completeness (IPaC for FWS). A better approach is needed given the fiscal, time, and personnel constraints the Services face. iConsult is an initial foray into better consultation.

Concept

The basic premise of iConsult is that we can use a mixed model of Service- "controlled" consultation and an interactive crowd-sourcing app to make consultations work better. On one hand, the ESA gives FWS and NMFS the responsibility for implementing section 7 consultations; the Services are ultimately answerable. On the other hand, over 93% of consultations from 2008-2015 were "informal" consultations, meaning they covered actions that were no likely to harm listed species. Even though they tend to go by pretty quickly, should professional biologists with the Services spend their time reviewing that many actions that don't harm listed species? If not, then how can the necessary compliance that protects species be ensured?
